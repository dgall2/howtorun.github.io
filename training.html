Fitness Trackers: Running Watch

In order to measure distance, pace, heart rate, and other data, fitness trackers like Garmin watches (below) have long been used. GPS and biometric technology can pinpoint where on your run you almost threw up.

Workouts: Fartleks, Tempos, Intervals

Steady runs can only get you so fit. In order to gain a larger training volume at a higher intensity, workouts like long-interval fartleks (which mean 'speed play') can be used.

Social Sites: Strava

What good is a gps watch and a long workout if you can't brag about it to your friends on Strava? Immediately find yourself surrounded by endless training posts with subliminal captions and a growing sense of competitiveness with your followers.

Recovery: Compression, Icing, Rolling, Stretching

Gains are made when the damage done during exercise is recovered and rebuilt. Injuries occur when training load exceeds recovery. Since this is key, runners can turn to a large assortment of tools in order to work out any soreness.

Crosstraining: Aquajogging, Lifting

A common word in running is mitigating 'impact'. To minimize impact on joints, alternative approaches such a aquajogging and lifting are used. If you thought running was boring, wait until you do it in a dingy pool, staring at a wall the whole time.
